# Objective

No on-call rotation or post-incident review process exists -- SOC2 CC7.2 gaps. The COMPLIANCE.md document identifies these as P2 remediation items under Incident Response. Create documentation that defines an on-call rotation schedule template, a severity-based escalation matrix, and a post-incident review (PIR) template with a regular cadence. These documents provide the operational framework required for SOC2 CC7.2 compliance.

# Files

- `docs/ON-CALL.md` (new) -- on-call rotation schedule, escalation matrix, responsibilities
- `docs/PIR-TEMPLATE.md` (new) -- post-incident review template with root cause analysis
- `docs/COMPLIANCE.md` (update) -- mark CC7.2 on-call and PIR gaps as addressed

# Implementation Plan

1. Create `docs/ON-CALL.md` with sections:
   - **On-Call Rotation Schedule**: Weekly rotation template with primary and secondary on-call roles, handoff day/time, and roster format (table with name, start date, end date, role)
   - **On-Call Responsibilities**: What the on-call engineer is expected to do (monitor alerts, acknowledge pages, triage incidents, escalate when needed)
   - **Response Time SLAs**: Define expected response times by severity:
     - SEV1 (Critical): Acknowledge within 5 minutes, begin triage within 15 minutes
     - SEV2 (High): Acknowledge within 15 minutes, begin triage within 30 minutes
     - SEV3 (Medium): Acknowledge within 1 hour, begin triage within 4 hours
     - SEV4 (Low): Acknowledge next business day
   - **Escalation Matrix**: Severity-based routing table showing who gets paged, when to escalate, and to whom:
     - SEV1: On-call primary -> engineering lead (15 min) -> CTO (30 min)
     - SEV2: On-call primary -> engineering lead (30 min)
     - SEV3: On-call primary (no auto-escalation)
     - SEV4: Ticket queue (no page)
   - **Handoff Procedure**: What to communicate during rotation handoff (open incidents, known issues, upcoming deployments)
   - **Tools**: Reference PagerDuty/OpsGenie for schedule management and alert routing
2. Create `docs/PIR-TEMPLATE.md` with sections:
   - **Metadata**: Incident ID, date, severity, duration, author, attendees
   - **Timeline**: Chronological list of events from detection to resolution
   - **Impact**: Users affected, services impacted, data loss (if any), SLA breach (if any)
   - **Root Cause Analysis**: 5 Whys or fishbone diagram format
   - **Contributing Factors**: Non-root-cause items that made the incident worse or delayed resolution
   - **What Went Well**: Things that worked during incident response
   - **Action Items**: Table with description, owner, due date, and status
   - **Lessons Learned**: Key takeaways for the team
   - **PIR Cadence**: PIRs required for all SEV1 and SEV2 incidents within 3 business days; optional for SEV3; monthly review of open action items
3. Update `docs/COMPLIANCE.md`:
   - Under CC7.2, mark "on-call rotation and escalation path" gap as addressed (reference `docs/ON-CALL.md`)
   - Mark "post-incident review process" gap as addressed (reference `docs/PIR-TEMPLATE.md`)
